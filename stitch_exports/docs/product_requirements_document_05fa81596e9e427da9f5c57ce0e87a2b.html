Excellent! As a Product Manager, I'm ready to create a project PRD (Product Requirements Document) or a brief for you. To do that effectively, I need **"the following context"** from you. Please provide details about your project. The more information you give me, the more accurate and useful the PRD/brief will be. Here's a list of questions and types of information that would be incredibly helpful. You don't need to answer all of them, but try to give me as much as you can: --- ### **Context Gathering Questions (Please provide answers):** **1. Project Identification:** * **Project Name:** What do you want to call this project? * **Project Lead/Owner:** (Optional) Who is leading this? **2. The "Why": Problem & Opportunity** * **What problem are you trying to solve?** (For whom? What pain point exists?) * *Example: "Users struggle to find available meeting rooms quickly."* * **What is the core opportunity this project addresses?** (What value will it create?) * *Example: "Streamline meeting room booking, reduce wasted time, increase office efficiency."* * **What's the current situation or alternative?** (How do people do this now?) * *Example: "People walk around looking for rooms, use a clunky old system, or block calendars without real bookings."* **3. The "What": Solution & Scope** * **What is your proposed solution or product idea?** (High-level description) * *Example: "A mobile app and web portal for real-time meeting room booking."* * **Who is the primary target audience/user?** * *Example: "Office employees, facilities managers."* * **What are the key features or functionalities you envision?** (List them out, even if high-level) * *Example: "Search by room size/equipment, view room availability, book/cancel rooms, receive notifications."* * **What is definitely *out* of scope for the initial version (MVP)?** (What should we *not* build yet?) * *Example: "Integration with external calendars, catering orders, visitor management."* **4. Goals & Metrics:** * **What are the measurable goals for this project?** (How will you know it's successful?) * *Example: "Increase meeting room utilization by 15%, reduce booking time by 50%, achieve 80% user satisfaction."* * **What are the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) you'll track?** **5. Business Value:** * **How does this project align with overall company strategy or business objectives?** * **What business value does it bring?** (e.g., increased revenue, cost savings, improved customer satisfaction, market share, compliance) **6. Timeline & Resources (Optional but helpful):** * **Is there a desired launch date or timeframe?** * **Are there any known constraints (budget, resources, technology)?** **7. Other Context:** * **Any existing systems it needs to integrate with?** * **Any specific technologies or platforms in mind?** * **Any known risks or challenges?** --- **Once you provide the context, I will generate a PRD or brief structure that fits your needs. I'll generally default to a more comprehensive PRD that can be easily condensed into a brief.**
